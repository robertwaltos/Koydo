# Foundations of Gardening

## What Is Gardening?

Gardening is the practice of cultivating plants—flowers, fruits, vegetables, herbs, and ornamentals—in a planned outdoor or indoor space. Unlike large-scale agriculture, gardening typically occurs at a household or community level and emphasises personal care of the growing environment.

## Soil Basics

Soil is the foundation of every garden. It is a living ecosystem made up of minerals, organic matter, water, air, and billions of microorganisms.

### Soil Texture

Soil texture describes the proportion of sand, silt, and clay particles:

| Particle | Diameter | Character |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| Sand | 0.05–2 mm | Gritty, drains fast, low nutrient retention |
| Silt | 0.002–0.05 mm | Smooth, moderate drainage |
| Clay | < 0.002 mm | Sticky when wet, high nutrient holding capacity |

**Loam**—an approximately equal mix of sand, silt, and clay—is considered ideal for most garden plants.

### Soil pH

pH measures acidity or alkalinity on a 0–14 scale. Most garden plants thrive between pH 6.0 and 7.0. A simple home test kit or digital meter can measure soil pH. Lime raises pH; elemental sulphur lowers it.

### Organic Matter

Organic matter (decomposed plant and animal residues) improves soil structure, water retention, and nutrient availability. Adding compost, aged manure, or leaf mould increases organic matter content over time.

## Composting

Composting converts kitchen scraps and garden waste into nutrient-rich humus.

### The Carbon-to-Nitrogen Ratio

Effective composting balances carbon-rich "browns" (dried leaves, cardboard, straw) with nitrogen-rich "greens" (vegetable peelings, grass clippings, coffee grounds). A ratio of roughly 25–30 parts carbon to 1 part nitrogen by weight promotes fast decomposition.

### Hot vs. Cold Composting

- **Hot composting**: Actively managed pile reaching 55–65 °C; kills weed seeds and pathogens; finished in 4–8 weeks.
- **Cold composting**: Passive pile; lower temperatures; takes 6–12 months but requires less effort.

### Vermicomposting

Red wiggler worms (Eisenia fetida) break down food scraps in a contained bin. Worm castings are an exceptionally nutrient-dense amendment.

## Plant Nutrition

Plants require 17 essential elements. The three macronutrients are:

1. **Nitrogen (N)** — promotes leafy growth.
2. **Phosphorus (P)** — supports root development and flowering.
3. **Potassium (K)** — boosts disease resistance and fruit quality.

Secondary macronutrients (calcium, magnesium, sulphur) and micronutrients (iron, manganese, zinc, boron, copper, molybdenum, chlorine, nickel) are needed in smaller quantities.

### Fertiliser Labels

A bag labelled "10-10-10" contains 10 % nitrogen, 10 % phosphorus (as P₂O₅), and 10 % potassium (as K₂O) by weight.

## Watering Principles

- **Deep, infrequent watering** encourages roots to grow deeper.
- Water early morning to reduce evaporation and fungal risk.
- A rain gauge or moisture meter helps avoid over- or under-watering.
- Drip irrigation delivers water directly to root zones and conserves water compared to overhead sprinklers.

## Common Plant Diseases

| Disease | Cause | Symptoms | Prevention |
|---------|-------|----------|------------|
| Powdery mildew | Fungus | White powder on leaves | Air circulation, resistant varieties |
| Blight | Oomycete | Brown lesions, rapid wilting | Crop rotation, remove debris |
| Root rot | Overwatering / fungi | Yellowing, wilting despite wet soil | Good drainage, avoid overwatering |
| Mosaic virus | Virus (aphid vector) | Mottled leaves, stunted growth | Control aphids, remove infected plants |

## Seasonal Planning

A garden calendar helps align planting, maintenance, and harvest with local climate:

1. **Spring** — soil preparation, seed starting, transplanting frost-tender seedlings after last frost date.
2. **Summer** — watering, mulching, pest monitoring, successive sowing of salad crops.
3. **Autumn** — harvesting, planting cover crops, composting fallen leaves.
4. **Winter** — planning next season, maintaining tools, protecting perennials with mulch.

## Companion Planting

Certain plant combinations benefit each other:

- **Tomatoes + Basil** — basil may repel aphids and improve tomato flavour.
- **Carrots + Onions** — onion scent deters carrot fly.
- **Beans + Corn + Squash** (Three Sisters) — beans fix nitrogen, corn provides a trellis, squash shades soil.

## Raised Beds and Container Gardening

Raised beds offer improved drainage, warmer soil in spring, and fewer weed problems. Containers allow gardening on balconies and patios. Both require more frequent watering than in-ground beds.

## Sustainable Practices

- **Mulching** suppresses weeds, conserves moisture, and moderates soil temperature.
- **Crop rotation** prevents nutrient depletion and breaks pest cycles.
- **Integrated Pest Management (IPM)** combines biological, cultural, and mechanical controls before resorting to chemical treatments.
- **Water harvesting** (rain barrels, swales) reduces reliance on mains water.
