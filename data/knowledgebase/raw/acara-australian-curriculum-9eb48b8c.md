                 When was the Australian Curriculum developed?

 In 2008, all Australian governments agreed a national curriculum was needed to deliver an equitable, quality education for all young Australians.  The first Australian Curriculum was released in 2010 for English, Mathematics, Science and History Foundation to Year 10 (Version 1.0). By 2015, all learning areas and subjects had been progressively added to the F–10 Australian Curriculum.&nbsp;</p>\r\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\r\n<p>Read more about the history of the Australian Curriculum&nbsp;<a href=\&#34;https://www.acara.edu.au/curriculum/history-of-the-australian-curriculum\&#34;>here</a>.</p>\r\n&#34;}}" id="text-0d0c25c73f" class="cmp-text ">
     In 2008, all Australian governments agreed a national curriculum was needed to deliver an equitable, quality education for all young Australians.  The first Australian Curriculum was released in 2010 for English, Mathematics, Science and History Foundation to Year 10 (Version 1.0). By 2015, all learning areas and subjects had been progressively added to the F–10 Australian Curriculum.

 Read more about the history of the Australian Curriculum  here .

                 What changed from Version 8.4 to Version 9.0?

 In 2020–21 ACARA undertook a review of the F–10 Australian Curriculum at the direction of education ministers. Revisions were made to all 3 dimensions of the Australian Curriculum as a result of an extensive consultation and review process. In April 2022, education ministers approved revisions to the F–10 Australian Curriculum (Version 9.0) replacing the current curriculum (Version 8.4).&nbsp;</p>\r\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\r\n<p>Overall, from Version 8.4 to Version 9.0, content was revised and realigned to ensure it was up-to-date, had a strong evidence base and matched the high standards expected in other high-performing countries. Content was also reduced and refined to improve useability and implementation, especially for primary school teachers.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>\r\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\r\n<p>Read more about the changes from Version 8.4 to Version 9.0&nbsp;<a href=\&#34;https://v9.australiancurriculum.edu.au/resources/stories/curriculum-changes\&#34;>here</a>.</p>\r\n&#34;}}" id="text-46c7b70909" class="cmp-text ">
     In 2020–21 ACARA undertook a review of the F–10 Australian Curriculum at the direction of education ministers. Revisions were made to all 3 dimensions of the Australian Curriculum as a result of an extensive consultation and review process. In April 2022, education ministers approved revisions to the F–10 Australian Curriculum (Version 9.0) replacing the current curriculum (Version 8.4).

 Overall, from Version 8.4 to Version 9.0, content was revised and realigned to ensure it was up-to-date, had a strong evidence base and matched the high standards expected in other high-performing countries. Content was also reduced and refined to improve useability and implementation, especially for primary school teachers.

 Read more about the changes from Version 8.4 to Version 9.0  here .

                 What changes have been made to the Australian Curriculum?

 The Australian Curriculum Version 9.0 includes the following key changes:</p>\r\n<ul>\r\n<li>improved consistency and clarity of language in achievement standards and content descriptions</li>\r\n<li>strengthened cognitive alignment between content descriptions and achievement standards</li>\r\n<li>refined content descriptions to provide greater clarity to teachers about what to teach</li>\r\n<li>removed and de-emphasised some content, while other content has been added or given more emphasis</li>\r\n<li>revised content elaborations to provide authentic examples for teachers, which identify opportunities to develop general capabilities and cross-curriculum priorities</li>\r\n<li>improved level descriptions to provide teachers with a clear overview of the learning that students should experience in each level or band</li>\r\n<li>revised and strengthened general capabilities and cross-curriculum priorities.</li>\r\n</ul>\r\n&#34;}}" id="text-78a9f6ad82" class="cmp-text ">
     The Australian Curriculum Version 9.0 includes the following key changes:

  improved consistency and clarity of language in achievement standards and content descriptions
 strengthened cognitive alignment between content descriptions and achievement standards
 refined content descriptions to provide greater clarity to teachers about what to teach
 removed and de-emphasised some content, while other content has been added or given more emphasis
 revised content elaborations to provide authentic examples for teachers, which identify opportunities to develop general capabilities and cross-curriculum priorities
 improved level descriptions to provide teachers with a clear overview of the learning that students should experience in each level or band
 revised and strengthened general capabilities and cross-curriculum priorities.

     State and territory education authorities and non-government sectors will determine the timeline and approach to implementation of Version 9.0 in their jurisdiction and will communicate that to their schools.

 Read more about state and territory implementation  here .

                 Where can I find the Australian Curriculum for senior secondary?

 By 2013 ACARA had developed 15 senior secondary subjects across English, Mathematics, Science, History and Geography as the agreed and common base for the development of state and territory senior secondary courses.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>\r\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\r\n<p>At this point in time, there are no future plans for ACARA to develop further senior secondary Australian Curriculum subjects. You can find the 15 senior secondary subjects&nbsp;<a href=\&#34;https://www.australiancurriculum.edu.au/senior-secondary-curriculum/\&#34;>here</a>.&nbsp;</p>\r\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\r\n<p>The senior secondary Australian Curriculum for each subject specifies content and achievement standards. State and territory curriculum, assessment and certification authorities are responsible for determining how the Australian Curriculum content and achievement standards are to be integrated into their courses. The state and territory authorities also determine assessment and certification specifications for their courses and any additional information, guidelines and rules to satisfy local requirements, including advice on entry and exit points and credit for completed study.&nbsp;</p>\r\n&#34;}}" id="text-5eaeb8cf43" class="cmp-text ">
     By 2013 ACARA had developed 15 senior secondary subjects across English, Mathematics, Science, History and Geography as the agreed and common base for the development of state and territory senior secondary courses.

 At this point in time, there are no future plans for ACARA to develop further senior secondary Australian Curriculum subjects. You can find the 15 senior secondary subjects  here .

 The senior secondary Australian Curriculum for each subject specifies content and achievement standards. State and territory curriculum, assessment and certification authorities are responsible for determining how the Australian Curriculum content and achievement standards are to be integrated into their courses. The state and territory authorities also determine assessment and certification specifications for their courses and any additional information, guidelines and rules to satisfy local requirements, including advice on entry and exit points and credit for completed study.

                 Is a specialist teacher needed to teach HPE, Science, Technologies or The Arts in primary years?

 The Australian Curriculum allows teachers, including classroom and specialist teachers, to implement the curriculum to meet the needs of their students. It also enables schools to partner with organisations to deliver programs that complement teacher-led learning.</p>\r\n&#34;}}" id="text-04bb63939f" class="cmp-text ">
